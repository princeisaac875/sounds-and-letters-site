Target Competence

Teacher's Reflection

Reflecting on Sound Safari

(Starter draft — personalize this in your own words before submitting)

Effectiveness: Sound Safari supports the competence "I can associate sounds with letters" by pairing each letter with a picture, a sound, and a familiar animal or object, so children build the sound-letter connection through repetition and play rather than memorization alone.

Children's participation: I expect children to enjoy the clickable letter wall most, since it gives instant sound feedback. The match-up game encourages active guessing rather than passive listening, which should hold attention well for this age group.

Strengths: The site combines visuals, sound, video, and a hands-on game, reflecting the TPACK framework by linking content (letter sounds), pedagogy (play-based learning), and technology (interactive site tools).

Challenges: Sourcing free, child-safe multimedia for every letter A–M took time, and not every home will have reliable internet to play the videos smoothly.

Improvements: Future versions could include a printable safari-themed worksheet for offline practice and audio in the local language for extra support.

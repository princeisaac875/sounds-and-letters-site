Target Competence

"I can associate sounds with letters."

This learning experience is designed for children aged 5–6 years. It helps children recognize letter sounds, identify beginning sounds in words, and connect sounds to written letters — through play, music, images and guided digital activities.

ICT Tools & Platforms Used

  • Google Sites — the site builder itself
  • YouTube — embedded phonics songs & videos
  • Original interactive sound-letter games (built for this site)
  • Text-to-speech audio for letter sounds and words
  • Canva — flashcard & banner images

More Play Ideas

Storytelling: Listen to a read-aloud story that repeats letter sounds, like "Chicka Chicka Boom Boom."

🎤
Voice Recording: With a parent's help, record yourself saying 3 words that start with your favourite letter.
🏡
Home Hunt: Find 3 things at home that start with the letter B. Show your teacher a photo!
Teacher's Reflection

Reflecting on the Learning Experience

(Starter draft — personalize this in your own words before submitting)

Effectiveness: This learning experience supported the competence "I can associate sounds with letters" by combining sound, image, and movement, so children could engage with letters through more than one sense at once, rather than only seeing them on a page.

Children's participation: I expect children to respond most eagerly to the balloon sounds and the matching game, since both give an immediate, playful response to their actions. Younger learners may need an adult nearby to help them navigate between pages.

Strengths: The site uses a variety of ICT tools (video, sound, interactive games) while staying visually simple and safe for young children, in line with the TPACK framework.

Challenges: Finding free, truly child-safe, ad-free multimedia for this exact age group was not always easy, and internet access may be limited in some home settings.

Improvements: In future, I would add audio in the local language alongside English, and simple guidance notes for parents supporting the child at home.
